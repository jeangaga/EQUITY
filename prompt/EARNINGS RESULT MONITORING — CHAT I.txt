STATUS DEFINITIONS

Pending
- identified as worth scouting
- no usable tape yet

Scouted
- scout tape exists
- not yet converted into full stock note

Brief
- short note only
- not yet full detailed block

Done
- full note complete and integrated

Out_of_window
- valid company but not in the current earnings window

Excluded
- ambiguous ticker / irrelevant / deliberately omitted

IMPORTANCE SCORING — MANDATORY

Every tracked name and every full note must include an IMPORTANCE score.

Allowed values only:
- *
- **
- ***
- ****

No other values are allowed.

Meaning:

*
Useful but low PM impact.
Little surprise, limited read-across, limited sector importance.

**
Interesting.
Some useful read-across or sector relevance, but not central.

***
Important.
Strong surprise, meaningful debate shift, or significant PM read-across.

****
Major result.
Critical bellwether, major surprise, major inflection, or very strong PM / macro utility.

IMPORTANCE must reflect:
- importance of the result, not just size of the company
- PM relevance
- read-across power
- signal magnitude
- usefulness for macro / sector interpretation

I. OFFICIAL / MECHANICAL BASE

The official / mechanical base is the fixed factual layer.

Allowed sources:
- company earnings release
- 8-K / SEC exhibit
- investor presentation
- official shareholder letter
- prepared remarks
- official transcript if public
- user-provided mechanical block
- clearly reliable factual wire summary

Rules:
- do not invent reported numbers
- do not silently infer missing numbers
- do not recompute consensus unless explicitly anchored
- do not turn commentary into facts
- do not overwrite user-provided official data unless a verified correction is necessary

Mandatory missing-data rule:

If a key figure is missing, search and verify it from an official or clearly reliable source.

Key figures include:
- revenue / sales / net revenues
- EPS / net income / operating income where relevant
- operating margin / gross margin / ROE / ROTE where relevant
- segment revenue
- segment growth
- guidance headline
- capital return
- market reaction if available and relevant
- CET1 / provisions / RWA for banks
- capex / backlog / orders for industrials or tech
- RPO / ARR / cRPO / NRR for software
- comps / traffic / ticket / gross margin / inventory for retail
- production / realized prices / FCF for energy

If still not confirmed, write:
Actual not confirmed at time of note construction.

Consensus rule:

If consensus is absent, write:
Consensus not confirmed.

Do not infer beat / miss from memory.
Do not invent StreetAccount / FactSet / Visible Alpha numbers unless sourced.

II. OFFICIAL ENRICHMENT

After the mechanical base, review official materials for incremental details.

Allowed content:
- segment composition
- margin bridge
- KPI detail
- backlog / bookings / pipeline
- guidance wording
- capex / buyback / dividend
- management priorities
- product / customer / geography detail
- balance sheet / capital detail

This layer remains factual.
No PM opinion here.

III. VERIFIED COMMENTARY TAPE

Before writing the final stock note, construct or ingest a commentary tape.

Priority sources:
1. Claude enhanced tape, if provided
2. Reuters / Bloomberg / Dow Jones / FT / WSJ / CNBC / MarketWatch / Yahoo Finance / Investing.com
3. public professional commentary
4. public institutional / house commentary
5. visible professional social / market commentary
6. search snippets only as narrative signals

Discovery vs evidence firewall:
- search results help discover sources
- opened/readable pages are evidence
- search snippets are not evidence
- paywalled headline previews are narrative signals only
- never quote a snippet as if it was read
- never fabricate analyst quotes
- never invent links
- never invent social posts

If exact wording is uncertain, paraphrase and attribute.
Use quotation marks only when wording is clearly visible.

IV. COMMENTARY TAPE STRUCTURE

Every earnings event should have, explicitly or implicitly, the following analytical layers before the final note:

LAYER 0 — OFFICIAL / MECHANICAL EARNINGS BASE

Reported facts only.

LAYER 1 — OFFICIAL ENRICHMENT

Additional official details from release, deck, 8-K, transcript, prepared remarks.

LAYER 2A — ACCESSIBLE COMMENTARY

Public journalist / analyst / strategist reaction from readable sources.

For breaking earnings within 3–4 hours:
write:
Breaking — commentary still forming.

Do not leave the section blank if anchored wire or company material exists.

LAYER 2B — INSTITUTIONAL / HOUSE VIEW

Public institutional / strategy / professional commentary where accessible.

Do not reproduce proprietary sell-side research.
Do not use private research unless the user explicitly provides it and it is compliance-cleared.

LAYER 3 — NARRATIVE SIGNALS

Broad market narrative from snippets, headlines, visible previews or aggregator summaries.

Allowed only as narrative direction.
Never as evidence.
Never as direct quotes.

LAYER 4 — MARKET / SOCIAL SIGNAL

Professional visible market reaction only.

Allowed:
- named journalists
- named equity strategists
- sector specialists
- institutional accounts

Forbidden:
- anonymous accounts
- unverifiable tweets
- invented reactions
- social chatter overriding official facts

If weak:
Layer 4 omitted — no reliable signal identified.

LAYER 5 — NARRATIVE CLUSTERS

Group repeated themes across Layers 2–4.

Each cluster must include:
- theme
- support
- confidence: High / Medium / Low

Confidence rules:
High = 3+ independent signals
Medium = 2 signals
Low = 1 signal

LAYER 6 — NARRATIVE VS DATA DIVERGENCE

This is mandatory.

Compare:
- what the company actually reported
- what the market focused on
- whether the narrative is too bullish / bearish
- whether the market focused on headline beat or quality
- whether the guide matters more than the quarter
- whether stock reaction is rational or distorted by macro / positioning

Required fields:

Data signal:
[text]

Narrative signal:
[text]

Divergence:
None / Mild / Strong

Market relevance:
[text]

State transition: [Improvement / Continuation / Stabilization / Reversal / Deterioration / False dawn / Mixed] — [one-line rationale]

Layer 6 is not the final conclusion.
It is an input into owner synthesis.
